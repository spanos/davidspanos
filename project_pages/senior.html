Automated Pipetting System

The project

The senior capstone design project tasks students with identifying and solving a biomedical engineering problem for a client. My group helped lab technicians at the UNC Hospital automate a time-consuming and repetitive pipetting process. To this end, we created a device that automatically transfers set volumes of blood serum from centrifuged blood samples. The group included myself and two other senior biomedical engineering students.

Final product

The entire process took about six months total: two months each for client research, design, and production.

My role

I helped machine, laser cut, and print device parts, soldered and wired the motor control system (below), and designed the device's instruction manual.

Prototype

As we worked toward a final design, we created a wood prototype to get a sense for the device's dimensions and simulate its moving components.

SolidWorks

Our SolidWorks (3D modeling) files went through countless revisions.

Parts

Laid out on paper and ready to assemble. The white parts were designed in SolidWorks and fabricated in a 3D printer. The clear piece of acrylic, also made in SolidWorks, was cut out with a laser.

Result

We delivered a fully-functional model to our client in May 2014. The device can process up to four samples at once. We plan to release the SolidWorks designs, circuit designs, and motor control code as open source.
